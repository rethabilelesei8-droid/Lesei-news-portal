Lesotho Government Unveils New Economic Recovery Strategy for 2026

By Rethabile Lesei 📅 March 22, 2026 ⏱ 5 min read 📍 Maseru

Senior ministers at the Maseru conference hall, presenting the five-year economic plan. Photo: Lesei's News Portal

Senior ministers gathered in Maseru this week to present a comprehensive five-year economic recovery plan, aimed at reducing unemployment and attracting foreign direct investment into the Kingdom of Lesotho. The announcement came after months of consultations with business leaders, civil society, and international partners.

The plan covers five key sectors: agriculture, manufacturing, tourism, digital services, and education. Officials say the strategy is designed to create over 30,000 new jobs by 2030, with a focus on young people and women in rural communities.

"This plan is not just about numbers — it is about real change for real people in every corner of our country."

— Minister of Finance, speaking at the Maseru launch event

Key Highlights of the Plan

Among the major announcements was a commitment to expand irrigation infrastructure in the lowlands to support small-scale farmers. Additionally, a new industrial park near Maseru is expected to attract light manufacturing companies from South Africa and beyond.

The government also announced partnerships with two international technology companies to provide digital skills training to over 10,000 young Basotho within the next two years.

In Pictures

Opposition parties have expressed cautious optimism, calling for strong accountability mechanisms to ensure the funds reach their intended beneficiaries. Several civil society groups have also called for public consultations in rural districts before implementation begins.
